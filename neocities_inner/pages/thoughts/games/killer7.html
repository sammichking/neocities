Before I try to tackle what I think happened, I'll talk about some other less confusing things

I wouldn't say that this was a direct evolution of the fixed camera stuff that was going on on the ps1, more like it sort of stepped off of it, like it's not just the same third person angle all the time, it changes around often enough. What is interesting though is that the most common camera angle is close to the ground looking up at the player from a couple meters back, whether or not this was chosen for the kaede shots or for other reasons, it's a interesting choice as the lack of visual information about where you're going keeps you tense and from falling into a simple rhythm. There's quite a few of things like that throughout, the method of movement and navigation is unlike anything I've ever played, although maybe that hinges more on the qualifier than the systems themselves, but although they are peculiar they work well and the game is fun. The puzzles are mainly pretty lame; go to place, is it a character ability? is it a ring? is it copy numbers from before? or is it poorly communicated bullshit, not much in the inbetween zone of good puzzle but there are a couple.

I should've been drinking more while playing this game, something to soothe me while iwazaru's grating ass voice said the same catchphrase for the 20th time that stage before flipping a coin on whether or not he's gonna say something funny or just tell me the solution to the puzzle. The music was largely great whenever the loud as fuck audio wasn't drowning it out, but the laugh noise was mixed so loud for a reason (so it scary).
